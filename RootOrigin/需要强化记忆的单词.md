| 单词                                             | 解释                                                         |
| ------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **oar**<br />【ɔːr】                             | a long pole with a flat blade at one end that is used for rowing a boat<br />船桨;铝合金桨;划桨。<br />oarfish:桨鱼;勒氏皇带鱼<br />**put/stick your oar in**: 多管闲事；横插一杠子<br />The trustworthiness is like the barge-pole oar of life ship, controlling the direction of life. |
| **orc**<br />【ɔrk】                             | an imaginary creature like a very ugly, violent human, that is described in books by J.R.R. Tolkien

| **galley**<br />【ˈɡæli】                        | **galley**  n.单层甲板的桨帆船。地中海风向多变，船如果光有帆，逆风或无风怎么办？用桨。但是用桨吧，水手得24小时划船，因为不划桨船就不动。另外，划桨前进的速度无论如何如何是太快不了的。怎么办？桨帆并用的galley船应运而生，但词源unknown，这也说明古代地中海地区确实有很多古代文明。 |
| **corridor**<br />【ˈkɔːrɪdɔːr】                 | 走廊(一国领土通过他国境内的狭长地带);(建筑物内的)过道;通道;(火车上的)走道;空中走廊(一国领空中允许他国飞机经过的区域)：a long narrow passage in a building, with doors that open into rooms on either side。<br />the corridors of power：(sometimes humorous) 权力走廊（高层政治决策机构） the higher levels of government, where important decisions are made.<br />You'll find his office just along the corridor. |
| **secular**<br />【ˈsekjələr】                   | 现世的；世俗的；非宗教的：not connected with spiritual or religious matters<br />(of priests 司祭) 教区的；在俗的：living among ordinary people rather than in a religious community<br />He spoke about preserving the country as a secular state.(他谈及了保持该国世俗化的问题。)<br />Mexico is a secular state and does not have diplomatic relations with the Vatican(墨西哥是个世俗国家，和梵蒂冈没有外交关系。) |
